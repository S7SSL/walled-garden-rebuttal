1. Executive Summary

This memorandum rebuts the Stirling Ackroyd valuation which assigned a land value of £0 to The Walled Garden. We defend a minimum land valuation of £1.5 million, supported by:

Key Defence Points:
  • Planning Certainty: Full planning consent granted (Sept 2024) removes regulatory risk
  • Local Comparable Sales: SG4 7TZ units trade at £795,000–£890,000 without planning upside
  • Aitchison Raffety Precedent: £500,000 (2023, pre-planning) → £1.5m (2026, post-planning) = defensible 3x uplift
  • Stirling Ackroyd Contradiction: Their cited comparable rate of £7,500/sqm directly contradicts the £0 land valuation

Conclusion: £1.5 million is a conservative, market-justified floor value.

4. Stirling Ackroyd's Internal Contradiction

Their Own Comparable Rate Contradicts the £0 Valuation

Stirling Ackroyd's valuation report cites 5 comparable sales and derives a comparable rate of approximately £7,500/sqm for land with improved properties.

The Problem:

If land is valued at £7,500/sqm in the local market, and The Walled Garden sits on approximately 2,000 sqm, the land value alone would be:

2,000 sqm × £7,500/sqm = £15,000,000

Yet Stirling Ackroyd assigned £0 to land.

This is internally contradictory. Either their comparable rate is wrong, or their £0 land valuation is indefensible. The evidence strongly supports the latter.

6. Owner-Occupier Self-Build Context

The Walled Garden is structured as an owner-occupier self-build site, not a speculative developer project. This is critical for valuation methodology:

  • No developer profit margin to extract
  • Build cost: £500–£600/sqft (Griggs Homes verbal quote, £600 realistic for premium Hertfordshire modular build)
  • GDV rate: £900/sqft (established on Dower House estate comparables)
  • Full scheme value: 6,500 sqft × £900/sqft = £5.85m GDV (pre-planning discount)

Given planning certainty now eliminates execution risk, £1.5m land floor is conservative relative to full scheme upside (£2.5m–£3.5m+).

7. Defended Valuation

MINIMUM LAND VALUATION

£1,500,000

This valuation is justified because it:

  1. Respects local precedent — Dower House comps £795k–£890k without planning
  2. Applies reasonable uplift — 3x from Aitchison 2023 baseline for planning certainty
  3. Sits midway — Between comparable unit prices and full scheme upside
  4. Uses conservative assumptions — £500/sqft build cost, £900/sqft GDV rate
  5. Accepts market risk — Does not extract full developer profit

Conclusion

We rebut Stirling Ackroyd's £0 land valuation and defend £1.5 million as a minimum, justifiable floor:

£1.5 million is defensible, market-tested, and appropriately prudent for lending purposes.
